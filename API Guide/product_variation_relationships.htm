Working with Product Variation Relationships

A product variation relationship is the relationship between a product and all available variations of that product.  For example, you might sell a specific type of bell-bottomed pants.  “Bell-bottomed Pants” would be the parent item (the generic identifier), and the blue, green, and red versions of those pants would be the child items.  Product variation relationships enable you to present all available versions of a product to your customers by linking the parent items with their respective children.  Examples of common variation themes include size, color, color & size or material.  Because they identify the generic version of a product, parent ASINs are not sellable items—only the variations (the blue, green, and red versions of the pants) are sellable items.
